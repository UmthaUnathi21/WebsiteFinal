Wireframes & Design Processes

Wireframes are a crucial part of the initial design phase for a screen design, i.e. the UI/UX for anything that has a screen - from mobile phones to kiosks to applications and websites. It is generally in this phase that any design flaws are found, and although any changes at this stage, in comparison to changes in the development phase, will be relatively cheap, it can highlight any flawed logic in the systematic layout of the design.

The wireframe serves as a blueprint for the website, outlining the placement of various elements such as headers, navigation menus, content areas, and footers. For my project, the wireframe laid out a clear plan for incorporating the dark blue background and the neon color palette (yellow, red, and green) for the headings, along with white for the body text. The choice of a pixelated font further enhances the retro gaming feel, providing a cohesive and immersive user experience.

Iterative Testing and Refinement

In the context of my project, usability testing involved assessing how effectively the neon color palette and pixelated font contributed to the overall user experience. Colleagues and potential users provided feedback on aspects such as readability, navigation ease, and the overall aesthetic appeal. This feedback is essential for identifying areas that need refinement.

They provide a visual blueprint that guides both design and development, ensuring that the final product is cohesive, functional, and aligned with the intended user experience. The iterative testing and refinement process, supported by wireframes, ensures that the design evolves based on feedback and usability insights, resulting in a website that is both engaging and effective.
